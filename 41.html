The Best Places to Request Free Wedding Invitation Samples

Weddings are expensive, so anything you can do to save money on your wedding, like getting free wedding invitation samples, can help.

Do you have a wedding coming up?

And if you do, are you feeling overwhelmed?

Don’t worry, you’re not alone.

The wedding registry website Zola surveyed 500 engaged and newlywed couples and found that 96 percent called wedding planning stressful! And 40 percent called it “very” or “extremely” stressful!

But planning everything, especially if you are planning for a wedding on a budget, can be a huge help in reducing stress.

One detail to plan for is finding the perfect wedding invitation.

How do you come up with the perfect design for your big day?

What if there was a way to get free wedding invitation samples?

Well, there is. In fact, there are many places you can get free wedding stuff like invitation templates for free. And in this post, we’ll show you how and where to get free wedding invitation templates and samples by mail and online.

Here are 23 ways to get free samples of wedding invitations.

Free Wedding Invitation Templates Online

Trying to find the right design for your invitation can be daunting.

There are so many things to consider (font, color, background, etc.).

Free Printable Wedding Invitations Templates

Here are 16 sites that offer free samples of their wedding invitations.

1. Wedding Chicks

WeddingChicks.com is a website dedicated to giving your dream wedding without a nightmare of a price tag.

They offer a variety of services, such as a list of vendors that sell wedding dresses, musicians, florists, photographers and more. They also offer dozens of templates of free wedding invitation samples.

They also offer ideas for save the date cards and thank you cards.

There are dozens upon dozens of downloadable templates for you to choose from and print out to show around.

And best of all, they’re all free.

2. Cards & Pockets

Cards & Pockets is a one-stop-shop for creating invitations or stationery for any event you can think of.

And yes, that list includes wedding invitations.

To see what they have, click on the “Wedding” header, then under “Wedding Invitations” click on “Free Templates.”

All told they have 35 really nice, and free, templates for you to choose from.

3. Download & Print

Download & Print is a website designed for you to create your own custom wedding invitation.

You add the names of the guests and other details.

Customize it by selecting the color scheme and font design.

Download and print the design then cut to the correct size.

In all, their website lists over 100 ideas to choose from!

Here is a link to test out some ideas and get some free samples to show your husband- or bride-to-be.

4. Greetings Island

Greetings Island is another website that is a destination for those looking for stationery ideas.

And they have quite a few ideas for wedding invitations.

Just click on “Invitations” at the top, then click on “Wedding.”

You will find hundreds of ideas to choose from.

And some of the invitation ideas come with matching RSVP cards.

And if you’re interested, they also have ideas for cards for bachelor parties, bridal showers, engagement parties, rehearsal dinners, and more.

5. Poster My Wall

Poster My Wall is a website for individuals and professionals to create their own posters, videos, graphics, and more

And one of their offerings is free templates for wedding invitations.

And if you’re struggling with ideas, you’re in luck.

They offer over 1000 designs for you to choose from!

They even offer over 100 save the date templates.

6. Canva

Canva is a website for both companies and individuals to create their own websites and stationary with a professional-looking touch.

When you first log on to the website you’ll see several pricing plans and options.

Don’t let that scare you off.

They have over 500 wedding invitation templates and over 3,000 save the date templates that are completely for free.

7. Printable Invitation Kits

Printable Invitation Kits offers up free and printable templates for any event you can think of.

And of course this includes wedding invitations and RSVP cards.

It’s a safe bet you’ll be able to find something you like.

After all, the site offers 31 pages of templates for you to choose from!

8. Love & Lavender

Love & Lavender offers a variety of templates for you to use for you wedding invitations and other stationary.

When you check out the site, you can see there are more than a few options to choose from.

To narrow down your list, use the different filters on the left-hand side of the page for you to narrow down your options ranging from style, color, and text.

But be aware some are free while others do have a price tag.

However, you can narrow your search to only the free options by using the price filter.

9. Avery

Avery.com gives you the opportunity to let your creative juices flow and create just about anything your imagination can come up with.

Not only can you make your own wedding invitations but also save the date cards, thank you notes, custom labels, and much more

All you have to do is go to the homepage and type “wedding” in the search bar, and you’ll get a look at what they have to offer.

10. Basic Invite

Yes, Basic Invite specializes in invitations.

But despite the name, they are anything but “basic.”

Click on the “Wedding” header at the top of the page.

From there you’ll see all the wedding invitations they have to offer.

But they also have guest cards, bridal shower cards, save the date cards, enclosure cards, menu cards, and more.

They also offer a free printable wedding planner and even a free website for your wedding.

That is anything but basic!

11. Purple Trail

Purple Trail is another website designed to allow people to let their creative juices flow and create their own custom stationary and invitations.

They have ideas for every event, including wedding invitations.

12. Minted Weddings

Minted is a design marketplace dedicated to connecting consumers and independent artists, giving these artists a chance to show their work and grow their brand.

One of their area of expertise is weddings.

They have a special section of their website called Minted Weddings where you can see a sampling of what their artists have to offer.

The also offer a free sample kit of different wedding invitations for you to look at.

13. Invitations By Dawn

Invitations By Dawn is solely dedicated to helping couples find their perfect wedding invitation.

14. The Wedding Shop at Shutterfly

The Wedding Shop at Shutterfly offers just about everything you can imagine for couples as well as guests.

15. Vistaprint

Most of you are probably familiar with Vistaprint as the place to get business cards printed.

One area of emphasis you may not be aware of is printing wedding invitations.

16. Elli

Elli is another website designed to let couples create and customize their ideal wedding invitation.

Electronic Invitations

Maybe you want to go the environmentally friendly rout and skip the paper invitations and opt for digital invitations instead.

Are there places you can check out free samples for those as well?

You bet there are!

Here are four sites offering totally free electronic wedding invitation samples.

17. Evite

As the name implies, Evite doesn’t focus on paper invitations.

They allow users to create digital invitations for their special events or occasions.

And yes, this includes wedding invitations.

There are eight pages of designs to choose from.

But be aware some are free, while others do charge a fee.

The free offerings will clearly be labeled “free” below the picture.

18. Punchbowl

Punchbowl is another place to check out if you’re interested in electronic wedding invitations.

And they offer an extensive selection of wedding invitations to choose from.

They also have plenty of choices for bachelor, bachelorette, bridal shower, and rehearsal dinner invites.

But know this is a subscription service, and they offer a free seven-day trial.

After that, your subscription starts.

So mark your calendar to make sure you get everything finished before that day.

19. Wedivite

Wedivite is another place that allows you to create your own custom, digital wedding invitation for free.

But they also offer so much more than just invitations.

They offer numerous other tools to make your wedding day much easier.

Their other services include a wedding album, seating arrangements, directions, and even a way to offer song suggestions.

They even have their own planning app.

20. Paperless Post

Paperless Post also offers you a wide variety of electronic wedding invitations and more.

Just look for the “Wedding” header at the top of the page.

From there you’ll have the option of selecting not just invitations but also save the date cards, bridal shower invitations, rehearsal dinner invites, and thank you cards.

All of your options will be listed down the left side of the page.

And some of your options include Kate Spade, Anthropologie, Crate & Barrell, and many more.

Other Ways to Score Free Wedding Invitation Samples

31. Go to Bridal Fairs

Have you thought about going to a nearby bridal fair?

If not, you should.

It will certainly be worth your time

Many of the retailers will be offering free samples.

Walk around and see if anyone is offering any free samples of wedding invitations.

You never know what you may find.

And if you don’t know if there are any bridal fairs in your area, check this link.

Find your state, click, and see what’s on the calendar.

32. Collect invitations and Save the Date cards from your friends

Do you have any friends or coworkers getting married before your big day?

If so, collect the invitations and save the date cards you get either by mail or electronically.

If you see one you like, ask them where they found it.

Or just ask the newlyweds or the soon-to-be-married couples around the office, the gym, or wherever if they have any extra invitations lying around.

If they do, ask them if you can have one and if you like it ask where they got it.

33. Ask the businesses around town

Do any business around your hometown print wedding invitations or save the date cards?

If so, do they give away samples of previous jobs?

It doesn’t hurt to ask.

If you’re not sure if anyone around town does this, do a quick internet search.

For example, a Google search of “Where To Get Wedding Invitations Printed in Washington, DC” turned up this.

Look at the search results, check out their websites, and see what you like.

Then drop by or give them a call and see if they have any samples they can give away.

Again, it never hurts to ask.
